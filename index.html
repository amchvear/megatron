Light Tracking Robot

Welcome to the future of automation. A robot that follows the light!

component used

The Light Tracking Robot uses advanced light sensors to detect and track light sources, adjusting its movement accordingly. It moves toward brighter areas by adjusting its motors based on light intensity.

The robot leverages two light-dependent resistors (LDRs) that act as the "eyes." These LDRs measure the light intensity and send signals to a microcontroller, which adjusts the motors' speed to steer the robot toward the brightest light source.

Components Used

Light Sensors (LDRs)

These sensors detect light intensity to guide the robot's movement.

DC Motors

The motors control the wheels, adjusting speed and direction based on sensor input.

Microcontroller

It processes data from sensors and sends signals to the motors.

Power Supply

Powers the entire system, ensuring the robot runs smoothly.

Advantages

  • Autonomous light detection and movement.
  • Great educational tool for learning robotics and automation.
  • Cost-effective and easy to build.
  • Expandable for more complex projects like solar tracking systems.

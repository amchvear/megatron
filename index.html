Light Tracking Robot

A Light Tracking Robot intelligently detects and follows a light source, making it ideal for automated systems and educational projects.

How It Works

The Light Tracking Robot uses light-dependent resistors (LDRs) to detect light intensity. Based on the input from the LDRs, it moves toward the light source by adjusting the motor speeds.

The robot uses differential steering to navigate toward the light. It compares the light intensity on both sides, allowing it to turn in the direction of the stronger light source.

Components Used

  • Light-Dependent Resistors (LDRs)
  • DC Motors
  • Motor Driver IC
  • Arduino or Microcontroller
  • Power Supply
  • Chassis and Wheels

Advantages

  • Autonomous Light Detection
  • Real-time Response to Environmental Changes
  • Ideal for Educational Purposes
  • Easy to Build and Program

What is a Light Tracking Robot?

A light tracking robot is an autonomous device designed to follow a light source using sensors to detect light intensity and direction.

How It Works

The robot utilizes light-dependent resistors (LDRs) to sense light. The microcontroller processes this data, adjusting the motors to steer the robot towards the light source.

  • Light Sensors: Detect the intensity of light.
  • Microcontroller: Processes sensor data and controls movement.
  • Motors: Drive the robot based on sensor feedback.

Components

The essential components for building a light tracking robot include:

  1. Light sensors (LDRs)
  2. Microcontroller (like Arduino)
  3. Chassis and wheels
  4. Motors and motor driver
  5. Power supply (batteries)
  6. Wires and connectors
  7. Chassis materials (plastic, metal)

Applications

Light tracking robots can be utilized in various fields:

  • Educational tools in robotics classes
  • Robotic toys that engage children and promote STEM learning
  • Research applications in automation and robotics
  • Interactive art installations that respond to light
  • Remote-controlled devices in outdoor settings

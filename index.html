What is a Light Tracking Robot?

A light tracking robot is an autonomous device designed to follow a light source by utilizing various sensors to detect the intensity and direction of the light.

How It Works

The robot uses light-dependent resistors (LDRs) to sense light. The microcontroller processes this data, adjusting the motors to change the robot's direction accordingly.

  • Light Sensors: Detect the intensity of light.
  • Microcontroller: Processes sensor data and makes decisions.
  • Motors: Control the robot's movement based on sensor input.

Components

The basic components needed for building a light tracking robot include:

  1. Light sensors (LDRs)
  2. Microcontroller (like Arduino)
  3. Chassis and wheels
  4. Motors and motor driver
  5. Power supply (batteries)
  6. Wires and connectors
  7. Chassis materials (plastic, metal)

Applications

Light tracking robots can be utilized in various fields, such as:

  • Educational tools for robotics classes
  • Robotic toys that engage children
  • Research in robotics and automation
  • Art installations that respond to light
